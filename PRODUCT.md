# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Productivity-frustrated people who've tried todo apps and been burned by tasks that vanish at midnight or reset without warning. They land here with mild skepticism — they've seen the same promises before. They want something that quietly holds them accountable without lecturing them. Context: mobile-first, discovered the app organically or via App Store search, scanning quickly to decide if this is worth a download.

## Product Purpose

Karm is a minimal iOS task tracker built around one idea: unfinished work doesn't disappear — it carries forward to the next day until done. No accounts, no cloud sync, no gamification. Just a persistent daily list. Success means a visitor downloads the app within 60 seconds of landing.

## Brand Personality

Calm · Persistent · Honest

Voice is plain and direct. No exclamation points, no motivational copy, no feature lists. Karm doesn't need to sell itself with hype — the concept is the pitch. The Sanskrit etymology ("Karm is the Sanskrit word for work") is the brand's grounding detail: this is about work itself, not productivity theater.

## References

Bear and Craft (writing apps) — quiet confidence, typography-first, earns trust through restraint. The specific quality to borrow: they feel like a craftsperson made them, not a startup pitching to investors. The design doesn't announce itself.

## Anti-references

- **Generic App Store template**: screenshot mockups on gradient backgrounds, big "Download Now" CTAs, bullet-point feature grids — the pattern that makes every iOS app look interchangeable.
- **Productivity guru apps**: bright colors, achievement badges, gamification, motivational copy ("You're crushing it!"), confetti. Karm is not a coach.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the pitch.** The product's concept is strong enough. Don't dress it in marketing scaffolding — let the idea breathe.
2. **Earn trust, don't demand it.** No superlatives, no feature claims. Show what the app does in plain language and let users decide.
3. **Typography carries the brand.** Following the Bear/Craft influence: type hierarchy, spacing, and weight are the visual system. No hero imagery, no decorative elements competing for attention.
4. **One job, done.** The page has a single conversion path. Every element either supports the download decision or shouldn't exist.
5. **The etymology is the soul.** "Karm is the Sanskrit word for work" is not a footnote — it's the brand's grounding anchor. It separates this from every other task app.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. 4.5:1 contrast for body text on both dark and light themes (the page responds to `prefers-color-scheme`). Verify contrast on the muted text variants (--text-60, --text-45) — these are the highest-risk values. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations added.
